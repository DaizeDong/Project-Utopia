### 3. Project Concept Summary

**Project Otopia** is a minimalist, grid-based management simulation where a small world runs itself through autonomous agents and emergent group motion. All living entities are rendered as simple spheres, and the world is a tile grid where roads and buildings are represented as special tiles. The player does not micromanage individuals; instead, they shape the system by constructing buildings/roads and adjusting high-level workforce priorities based on global resource information.

The project focuses on three pillars: NPC AI / Behavior Trees, Swarm & Boids Modeling, and Pathfinding & Navigation. Player-controlled workers are autonomous agents driven by behavior trees with a shared blackboard. Each worker continually evaluates needs and goals (e.g., hunger, job assignment, carrying resources) and executes prioritized behaviors such as seeking food, harvesting resources, delivering goods to storage, or assisting construction. A lightweight “resource manager” reads global state (food/wood stockpiles, building availability, and player-defined target ratios) and automatically assigns workers to roles like farming, logging, hauling, or building. This creates the core “resource management” gameplay loop: the village reacts to shortages without manual unit control.

To achieve convincing large-scale motion, crowds and herds are simulated using **Boids** steering. Workers naturally form flowing convoys along roads, while neutral herds and NPCs move in groups, wander the map, gather near points of interest, and sometimes cause disruption. Neutral agents can randomly trade (visiting market/storage tiles) or sabotage (targeting a building tile to temporarily reduce production or destroy a structure), creating dynamic pressure the player must respond to.

For navigation, agents compute global routes with **A\*** on the tile grid, then combine path-following with Boids forces for local collision avoidance and group coherence. Visually, the goal is to watch the settlement become a living system: resource flows appear as moving crowds, herds create organic motion, and the player’s building decisions reshape traffic patterns. A debug HUD (selected-agent state, current behavior label, resource counters, and optional path visualization) makes the AI interpretable and helps evaluate emergent behaviors in real time.
