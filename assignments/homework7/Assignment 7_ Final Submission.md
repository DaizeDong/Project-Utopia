# **Assignment 7: Final Project Submission & Post-Mortem**

**Prerequisite:** Completion of Assignment 6 (Feature Complete & Peer Playtesting).

## **1\. Overview**

Congratulations on reaching the final phase of CS 428/523\! In Assignment 6, you reached the "Feature Freeze" milestone and conducted a formal user study to gather critical feedback.

Assignment 7 represents the mandatory **Buffer Phase** and your **Final Release**. During this time, absolutely no new features or mechanics may be added. Your entire focus must be on polishing the existing experience, optimizing performance to meet your technical specifications, fixing bugs, and directly addressing the actionable feedback you gathered during your peer playtesting. The culmination of this assignment is a final polished build, a demo video, and an engineering retrospective (Post-Mortem).

## **1.5 Submission Requirements**

**Added by TA:** Please note that you do not need to beautify your report using LLMs. Reports should be clear, concise and comprehensively reflect your effort on the implementation. It should perform as a tool to help us understand your idea and code.

**Report Format:** Markdown files.

## **2\. Phase 1: The Buffer Phase & Polish**

This phase is dedicated to turning your feature-complete software into a robust, finished product.

* **Bug Fixing & Stability:** Prioritize game-breaking bugs and crashes. The final submission must run flawlessly from start to finish.  
* **Optimization:** Ensure your project maintains the target frame rate defined in Assignment 2\. Optimize your shaders, memory management, and rendering pipelines.  
* **Applying Playtest Feedback:** Implement the 3-5 action items you identified in your Assignment 6 User Study. If users were confused by the UI, fix the UI. If a specific interaction caused severe lag, optimize or adjust that interaction.  
* **Aesthetic Polish:** Finalize your lighting, post-processing, audio mixing, and UI alignment.

## **3\. Phase 2: The Final Demo Video**

You must create a final, polished video demonstrating your project.

* **Duration:** 2 to 4 minutes.  
* **Content:** \* A clear showcase of the complete, uninterrupted "Game Loop" (Start menu \-\> Core Experience \-\> End state).  
  * A technical breakdown segment highlighting your 2+ core pillars in action (e.g., visually demonstrating your custom physics, procedural generation, or advanced rendering techniques).  
  * Voiceover or clear text overlays explaining the technical achievements on screen.

## **4\. Phase 3: The Engineering Retrospective (Post-Mortem)**

Your final written deliverable is a comprehensive reflection on the semester-long development cycle.

* Added by TAs: A **very brief overview** of your two pillars and achievements.  
* **Playtest Resolution:** Detail exactly how you addressed the feedback and action items from your Assignment 6 user study.  
* **Technical Post-Mortem:** \* What were the biggest architectural or engineering challenges you faced?  
  * Did you have to pivot or cut major features from your original Assignment 2 MVP definition? Why?  
* **AI Tool Evaluation:** Reflect on your use of AI tools (generation, coding, etc.) throughout the semester. Did they speed up your workflow, or did debugging AI hallucinations cost you time?
