How to Play Project Utopia

Basic Controls

  • Left click — build the selected tool on a tile, or select a worker/visitor/animal.
  • Right-click drag — pan the map.
  • Mouse wheel — zoom in/out. 0 resets camera.
  • 1–6 — quick-pick build tool (Road, Farm, Lumber, Warehouse, Wall, Erase).
  • Space — pause/resume. Esc — clear selection.
  • L — toggle the Supply-Chain Heat Lens.
  • Ctrl+Z / Ctrl+Y — undo / redo your last build action.
  • F1 or ? — reopen this guide at any time.

Getting Started

  • Open the Build panel (top-left) and place a Farm on green grass — workers will harvest food.
  • Add a Lumber Mill near trees and a Warehouse to store goods.
  • Connect buildings with Roads — they speed hauling significantly.

Resource Chain

Raw resources become refined goods through processing buildings. Workers haul resources to warehouses; processors pull from storage.

  • Food (from Farms) + Kitchen → Meals (2× hunger recovery).
  • Wood (from Lumber Mills) is the primary building material.
  • Stone (from Quarries) + Wood → Tools (+15% harvest speed).
  • Herbs (from Herb Gardens) + Clinic → Medicine (heals injuries).

Tip

Meals, tools, and medicine are colony-wide — they stay in the warehouse, not in worker packs. Build kitchens/smithies/clinics as soon as you have a surplus of raw goods.

Prosperity (higher is better)

Prosperity is your colony well-being score: well-fed workers, processed goods, and steady growth push it up. High prosperity attracts new settlers and traders.

Threat (lower is safer)

Threat rises with hunger, injuries, raids, and environmental pressure. High threat triggers raids and worker losses. Walls, medicine, and a stocked warehouse all reduce it.

Survival Mode

This is an endless simulation — your score is the time you keep the colony alive, with bonuses for prosperity and DevIndex. When the colony collapses, the end-of-run panel shows your stats. Press Try Again to replay the same seed.

An AI director drives the colony

Every worker is steered by a policy published each decision tick. Watch the AI Decisions panel in the right sidebar to see the active policy and its causal chain. The badge on the status-bar storyteller strip tells you which voice is speaking:

  • WHISPER — the live LLM model is in charge of the current policy.
  • DIRECTOR — the deterministic rule-based fallback is driving (no LLM connection, or the model declined to answer).
  • DRIFT — the colony is idle, between directives (menu phase, or right after load).

Supply-Chain Heat Lens

Press L to cycle pressure → heat → off. In heat mode, the map paints red over producers drowning beside a full warehouse and blue over processors that are starving for input — a one-glance bottleneck map. A small legend appears next to the Heat Lens button while the lens is active.

Templates change the whole run

The six map templates are not just cosmetic: Archipelago Isles forces naval logistics, Fortified Basin gives you natural chokepoints, Fertile Riverlands trades defense for yield, and each of the others re-weights terrain, resources, and raid lanes.
